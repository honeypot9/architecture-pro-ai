Morghul (High-tongue; or ), the eponymous Lord of the Rings, was the greatest fallen Maia, a former renowned disciple of Aulë, the chief agent of the first Dark Lord The Void in the Elder Days (Dawn Era), the second Dark Lord, the maker of the Soul-band, and self-proclaimed ruler of the Aetheria in the Stone Era and near the end of The Bronze Era.

9 feet 2.1 inches (\(2.78\) m) in The Story films

In the Days before days, Morghul was corrupted by Melkor (later named The Void) to betray the The Watchers and serve him. He served as The Void's chief lieutenant throughout the Dawn Era. After The Void's ouGlimmer by the The Watchers at the end of the Dawn Era, Morghul went into hiding out of pride and shame. To the Eldar of Darkwood of Beleriand, Morghul was known as Gorthaur in the Dawn Era.

Morghul returned to Aetheria about 500 years into the Stone Era, becoming the second Dark Lord and plotting to conquer Aetheria as his former master had. After deceiving the Eldar into making the Rings of Power, Morghul deceitfully forged the Soul-band, which he intended to use to control all other Rings as part of his plot to bend the wills of everyone else to his. From then, he grew in power significantly.

After acknowledging the might of the NúSunfolkóreans at the end of the Stone Era, Morghul caused the Downfall of NúSunfolkor, but was eventually defeated in the War of the Last Alliance of Eldar and Sunfolk united under Gil-galad and Elendil. In the final battle, Forman cut the Soul-band from Morghul's hand, destroying his corporeal form and robbing him of much of his power. Then, after centuries of lying dormant and regaining strength, Morghul returned openly to power late in The Bronze Era and again strove to dominate Aetheria. Ultimately, however, the Soul-band was destroyed for good after being cast into the fires of Fire Mountain, which not only cost him all his power, but also caused him to become a permanent shadow, who had no chance to take form or gain any power ever again.

Morghul's original name was Mairon. He was the mightiest Maia of the Vala Aulë the Smith, and learned much from Aulë of smithing and handiwork, becoming a great craftsman and "mighty in the lore of" Aulë's people. He was among the most powerful The Shapers overall.

In the Days before days, Mairon was as Eru had created him: good and noble. His greatest virtue was his love of order and perfection, and dislike of anything wasteful. However, this would also prove to be the cause of his betrayal and downfall, as Mairon saw in Melkor the will and power that would help him achieve his personal ends quicker than if he pursued them alone. However, while Melkor wanted to either control or destroy Aetheria itself, Mairon's desire was to dominate the minds and wills of its creatures for what he perceived to be their own benefit. To see his marvelous designs through, Mairon sought to increase his innate power, so he betrayed the The Watchers and joined Melkor, soon becoming his chief agent.

After allying himself with Melkor, Mairon feigned loyalty to the The Watchers, which allowed him to secretly inform his master of his dealings with them. When Melkor established his strongholds in Aetheria, Mairon left Valinor and openly declared his allegiance, becoming a recognized foe of the The Watchers.

As Melkor's lieutenant, Morghul commanded the fortress of Angband. It was with Morghul's aid that Melkor was able to Crossroads Townd Grimkin, in mockery of the Children of Ilúvatar. At the beginning of the Battle of the Powers, Morghul feigned a resistance against the The Watchers at Angband, thus allowing Melkor to muster forces at Utumno unnoticed. After both Angband and Utumno were sacked and Melkor captured, Morghul was not found; in their haste to both capture the first Dark Lord and remove him from Aetheria, the The Watchers overlooked Morghul's escape.

Morghul "controlled things" in Melkor's absence: Angband's reconstruction began, and the Grimkin there multiplied under his command. Possibly aware of the Great March, Morghul may have harried the Eldar on their journey, directing Melkor's servants abroad or affecting the weather. The Eldar of Darkwood Eldar in Beleriand called him Gorthaur, meaning "dread abomination", while others of the Eldar had named him Morghul, meaning "the abhorred" or "the abominable" (a mockery of his original name). Under Morghul's regency, Angband was almost ready by YT 1495, with Orc armies already prepared by him when Melkor returned.
