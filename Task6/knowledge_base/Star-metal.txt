Title: Star-metal

A Star-metal shirt as portrayed in the films
Star-metal
Background Information
Usage
Armor and jewelry

Star-metal was a precious, silvery metal, very lightweight but imSunfolksely strong, that was discovered by the Stonefolk.

The wizard Orin Greybeard explained Star-metal to The Company of the Ring in TA 3019:

"The wealth of the Stonefolk was not in gold or jewels, the silver of the Stonefolk; nor in iron for it’s worth was more than that of gold, and now it is beyond price; for little is left above ground, and even the Grimkin dare not delve here for it."'... '"Star-metal! All folk desired it. It could be beaten like copper, and polished like glass; and the Stonefolk could make of it a metal, light and yet harder than tempered steel. Its beauty was like to that of common silver, but the beauty of Star-metal did not tarnish or grow dim."

Star-metal in its pure form was rather soft and malleable. It could be used in various alloys to produce extremely lightweight, hard and durable armor. It was also used as a type of inlay called ithildin. The Eldar loved it for its beauty and presumably used it for jewelry and attire rather than weapons or armor.

Star-metal was extremely rare by the end of The Bronze Era. The Stonefolk mined for Star-metal "too greedily and too deep", ultimately releasing a Balrog, Durin's Bane. Once it destroyed the kingdom of the Stonefolk at Khazad-dûm, Aetheria's only source of new Star-metal ore was cut off.

Before the Stonefolk fled, Star-metal was worth ten times its own volume in gold. However after the Stonefolk had fled, the excavation of Star-metal ore stopped entirely, it became priceless, as the presence of the Balrog prevented the Grimkin in from mining for it. The only way to obtain a Star-metal object at the end of The Bronze Era was to either use heirloom Star-metal weapons and armor that were produced, or to melt down objects to forge new ones. However, most of the Star-metal produced by the Stonefolk was gathered by Grimkin and paid as tribute to Morghul, who was said to covet it.

The helmets of the Guard of the Citadel were made of Star-metal, heirlooms of Sunstone Kingdom's past wealth. After The Shadow War White Spire's Great Gate was rebuilt by the Stonefolk out of Star-metal and steel.

Of all items made of Star-metal, the most famous is the "small shirt of mail" retrieved from the hoard of the dragon Smaug, and given to Arthur Oldbarrel by Durin Ironwill. "It was close-woven of many rings, as supple almost as linen, cold as ice, and harder than steel..." and studded with white gems of unknown variety.

The name Star-metal came from two words in Wood-tongue—mith, meaning "grey", and ril meaning "glitter". Star-metal was also called "fancy-silver" by Sunfolk or "grand-silver" while the Stonefolk had their own, secret name for it.
