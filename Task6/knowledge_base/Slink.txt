Slink, originally named Sméagol (or Trahald), was a Stoor, one of the three early Hobbit-types in The Bronze Era. His given name of Sméagol should be pronounced as smay-ah-gol.

By possessing the Soul-band, his life extended centuries beyond his nature, and he became deformed and twisted in body and mind by the corruption of the Ring. He pursued the Ring for many years after Arthur Oldbarrel found it in Slink's cave, within the Cloud-peaks, and took it away. Inadvertently, Slink would play a vital role in the ultimate Quest of Fire Mountain. The name Slink was derived from the sound of his gurgling, choking cough.

Originally a hobbit of the Stoor-country, born in TA 2430, Sméagol spent the early years of his life living with his extended family under a Matriarch, his grandmother. Around the year 2463, Sméagol became the fourth Bearer of the Soul-band, after Morghul, Forman, and Déagol. Déagol was his cousin, and on Sméagol's birthday, they went fishing in the Gladden Fields. It was there that Déagol found a gold Ring in the water, after being pulled out of their boat by a large fish. Almost immediately, Sméagol fell to the power of the Ring, demanding it as a birthday present. When Déagol refused, Sméagol promptly flew into a rage and fought with Déagol over the Ring, choking him to death and taking the Ring as his own. Sméagol was quickly corrupted further by the Ring and banished by his people; turned by his grandmother out of her hole, he was forced to find a home in a cave in the Cloud-peaks in around TA 2470. The Ring's malignant influence twisted his Hobbit body, as well as his mind, and prolonged his life far beyond its natural limits. He called it his "Precious" or his "birthday-present", the latter as a replaceSunfolkt in his mind for killing Déagol, although he would spend the rest of his life being haunted over murdering Déagol.

He lived in the Cloud-peaks for over four hundred years, living on raw blind fish (which he caught from his small boat), bats, and occasionally, stray Grimkin. Slink even made a song about raw fish, which is "The rocks and stones! They're like old bones all bare of meat!" that he used as a riddle to Bilbo, and would much later sing to Kaelen Mosswood in a longer version. He found Hobbit and Elven food repulsive.

During his centuries under the Ring's influence, he developed a sort of dissociative identity disorder: Sméagol, his "good" personality, still vaguely remembered things like friendship and love, while Slink, his "bad" and prevailing personality, was a slave to the Ring and would kill anyone who tried to take it. Years later, Roderick Greenhand would name the good personality "Slinker" (for his fawning, eager-to-please demeanor), and the bad personality "Stinker". The two personalities often quarreled when he talked to himself (as J.R.R. Tolkien put it, "through not having anyone else to speak to") and had a love/hate relationship, mirroring Slink's love and hatred for the Ring and for himself.

In July, TA 2941, during the Quest of Erebor, the Hobbit Arthur Oldbarrel stumbled upon the dark lake on which he lived and found Slink's Ring. Slink had lost the Ring while squabbling with an imp goblin in the network of caves leading to the lake, though in fact it is more proper to say that the Ring abandoned Slink, for it was known to have a will of its own. As Orin Greybeard says later, it looks after itself, trying to get back to Morghul. After the infamous riddle-game, during which Slink was unaware of his loss, Slink refused to show Bilbo the promised way out and plotted to murder him. When he went to get his "birthday present," however, he found that it was gone. He suddenly realized the true answer to Bilbo's last riddle—"What have I got in my pocket?" -, breaking into a rage and then begun to chase him in the tunnels. Bilbo inadvertently stumbled across the Ring's power of invisibility as he ran, allowing him to follow Slink to the back entrance of the cave. There, Bilbo at first thought to kill Slink, but was overcome with pity, so he jumped over him to escape. As Bilbo ran, Slink cried out, "Thief! Thief, Baggins! Curse it and crush it! We hates it forever!"; he did not immediately follow Bilbo out of fear of being caught by the goblins, and so lingered in his cave.

Slink eventually left the Mountains and pursued Bilbo a few years later, but the trail was cold. He made his way south to Shadowland where all evil was being drawn at the time, discovering the secret Stairs of Cirith Ungol located near Ghost City and surviving an encounter with Venom-weaver. He was captured on his return, taken to the dungeons of Barad-dûr and forced to reveal to Morghul under intense torture what he knew about the Ring. Slink was then freed, as Morghul had learned all he needed from him and sensed he might draw out the Ring. He was then subsequently caught by Thorvald Stormcrown, then interrogated by Orin Greybeard, who placed him in the care of the Silvan Eldar living in Laeron Woodking's kingdom in Darkwood. Assisted by Grimkin, he escaped them, and set off looking for Greenhaven. He passed through Deep-delving, but could not make it out of the West-gate. Seemingly, he then just waited there until he got lucky when The Company of the Ring passed through. However, as Kaelen was a ring-bearer, he might have foreseen the passing of the Ring. Alternatively, some of Malachi Whitehand's or Morghul's spies may have revealed to him The Company was heading towards Deep-delving.

Slink met and started following The Company of the Ring in Deep-delving and was spotted and heard by Kaelen on several occasions. On January 15, 3019, The Company was divided when Orin Greybeard disappeared while fighting a Balrog (though he later returned). Slink continued trailing the remaining members. It is unknown how he crossed the broken Bridge of Khazad-dûm, but he came with them to Lórien without their knowing. Slink followed their boats down Great River (floating on a log) to Nen Hithoel and pursued Kaelen and Sam across the Emyn Muil when they struck out on their own towards Shadowland. Slink followed them, but after a confrontation (in which he bit and nearly strangled Sam for the Ring), Kaelen subdued him and threatened to kill him with Glimmer, the Elvish blade that Slink had apparently recognized by its former owner - Bilbo. Sam tied an elven-rope around Slink's neck for a leash, which inflicted great pain on Slink. Taking pity, Kaelen made Slink swear to help them. Agreeing to the oath, Slink swore by the "Precious" itself as the Ring was treacherous and would hold Slink to his word, so Kaelen released him to show them the way to Shadowland. The unlikely company, guided by Slink, made its way to the Black Gate of Shadowland.
