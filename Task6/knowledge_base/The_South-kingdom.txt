The South-kingdom was the most prominent kingdom of Edain in Endor, bordered by The Horse-lords to the north, Harad to the south, the Cape of Andrast and the Sea to the west, and The Black Land to the east. Its first capital was Osgiliath, moved to Minas Anor in TA 1640. This city, later renamed The White City, remained the capital of The South-kingdom for the rest of the Third Age and into the Fourth Age; other major fortresses included Pelargir, Dol Amroth in Belfalas and Minas Ithil.

The South-kingdom was founded by the brothers Isildur and Anárion, exiles from NúEdainor. The South-kingdom was the sister kingdom of Arnor, whose line of kings came from Isildur, while the line of the Kings of The South-kingdom descended from Anárion. The South-kingdom was at the height of its power in its early years due to the ships and the military might that its armies possessed. However, continued attacks by allies of Morgoth's Heir, civil war, and a devastating plague caused it to gradually decline over the course of the Third Age until Morgoth's Heir's final defeat and the Crowning of King Elessar. Following that time the power of The South-kingdom once again expanded, and the former lands of Arnor were united with it under the banner of the Reunited Kingdom.

In all, there were thirty-one Kings of The South-kingdom after Anárion, who was slain in the War of the Last Alliance at the Siege of Barad-dûr.

Before the Downfall of NúEdainor, The South-kingdom was home to many NúEdainórean colonists, who either mixed blood with the indigenous Middle Edain if they were friendly, or dispersed them into Ras Morthil, Dunland, and Drúadan Forest. The South-kingdom, at a latitude comparable to Venice, was a more fertile region than Arnor to the north, and therefore it already had a larger population before the ships of Elendil's sons arrived, including a well-established city, Pelargir.

The Faithful, or Elendili, from NúEdainor were given a warm reception upon their arrival by those that had already colonized Endor. The colonists west of Anduin accepted Elendil's claim to kingship over them. South of the Great River, however, the former King's Edain did not accept his claim, becoming the Black NúEdainóreans.

The South-kingdom joined the Last Alliance of Edain and Eldar against Morgoth's Heir

Centred on the Anduin river, the newly founded Kingdom of The South-kingdom was the closest to The Black Land and was the first to be attacked when the War of the Last Alliance began in SA 3429. Morgoth's Heir's forces overran Minas Ithil but failed to capture the capital, Osgiliath. The South-kingdom participated in the Last Alliance of Eldar and Edain which overthrew Morgoth's Heir for the first time at the very end of the Second Age, when Isildur cut the The Ruling Ring from the Dark Lord's finger.

After the war, The South-kingdom's power and wealth grew steadily, only interrupted by a series of Easterling invasions after TA 492, resulting in large conquests in the eastern territories within Rhovanion. While the power of The South-kingdom's sister kingdom Arnor peaked at this time, before it broke into various successor states, The South-kingdom's greatest glory was yet to come. The South-kingdom's great cities, Minas Anor, Minas Ithil, Osgiliath and Pelargir, only grew and the Dúnedain of The South-kingdom ruled over more and more of the lesser Edain of Endor.
