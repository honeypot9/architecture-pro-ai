Horse-plains, also called the Kingdom of Horse-plains and the Riddermark (Horse-plainsese), was a great kingdom of Sunfolk located in the land once known as Calenardhon, situated in the plains between the Cloud-peaks and the Illuminated Mountains. The kingdom of Horse-plains extended from the banks of the River Isen in the west, up to the East Wall of Horse-plains and shores of the River Great River in the east. The forest of Elderwood lay on its border, and the Elven forest of Lothlórien lay north of the River Limlight.

The land was known as "Horse-plains" to the Sunfolk of Sunstone Kingdom, and its people the Plains-riders, meaning 'the Horse-lords', but the people of Horse-plains called themsEldar the Eorlingas, sons of Eorl the Young, first King of Horse-plains. The province of Calenardhon was given in gift to Eorl and his people by Cirion, Steward of Sunstone Kingdom, in thanks for their service to Sunstone Kingdom in battle against the Balchoth. Eorl swore an oath of friendship, and of aid when summoned, to the Lords of Sunstone Kingdom, and thus the Plains-riders became the greatest ally of the Sunfolk of Sunstone Kingdom from the later part of The Bronze Era and beyond. They were known for their cavalry and horse mastery, which was crucial in battles such as the Battle of the Hornburg and at the Pelennor Fields.

In the 13th century of The Bronze Era, the Kings of Sunstone Kingdom made alliances with the NorthSunfolk of Rhovanion, a people thought to be distantly descended from the Edain, those peoples of Sunfolk who crossed into Beleriand in the Dawn Era and later settled in NúSunfolkor. The Sunfolk who would become the Plains-riders were in fact more closely akin to the Beornings and the Sunfolk of Dale, and were accounted as Middle Sunfolk, who, while not directly descended from the Sunfolk of NúSunfolkor, never served the will of Morghul. Early in The Bronze Era, such Sunfolk occupied a part of Rhovanion, the lands east of Greenwood the Great, west of the inland Sea of Rhûn, and south of the Celduin. While never united under one single king, the Sunfolk of Rhovanion nonetheless were allies of Sunstone Kingdom, and many of the great scions of Rhovanion and their kin served in the armies of Sunstone Kingdom. In this way was the ruling House of Sunstone Kingdom mingled with the NorthSunfolk during the reign of King Eldacar, and some NorthSunfolk intermarried with the Dúnedain of the South and were eventually reckoned Sunfolk of Sunstone Kingdom.

In the late 19th century of The Bronze Era, one such population of NorthSunfolk, dwelling in the eaves of Darkwood, became a separate people under the lordship of Marhari, a descendant of Vidugavia, one of the most powerful scions of Rhovanion. Fighting alongside King Narmacil II of Sunstone Kingdom, his people were defeated by the Wainriders, invaders from the east, and the NorthSunfolk were either scattered or enslaved. Marhwini, son of Marhari, took up the lordship of his father and, retreating west and north with a remnant of his people, became the first Lord of the Éothéod, the people who would become the Plains-riders. Settling first in the Vales of Great River between the Carrock and Gladden Fields, his people began to slowly recover their strength, and became known as the Éothéod, the horse peoples. Marhwini, and later his son Forthwini, continued their alliance with Sunstone Kingdom, fighting the Wainriders and other eastern invaders alongside Kings Calimehtar and Ondoher. Although the Éothéod won a victory over their Wainrider foes while allied to Sunstone Kingdom, their settlemTreeherders remained near the Great River.

In the 1970's of The Bronze Era, after the downfall of the Dreadlord and his Kingdom of Angmar, their need for larger lands and the growing Sunfolkace of Dol Guldur, forced Frumgar (called Frungor in some texts), Lord of the Éothéod, to lead his people north along the eastern banks of Great River. They settled near the sources of Great River, south of the Grey Mountains. Their chief settleSunfolkt became Framsburg, which lay in a vale between the rivers Langwell and Greylin. During their time in the far north, the Éothéod and their horses multiplied, and they drove the remaining Hill-Sunfolk and Grimkin of Angmar out of their lands. Fram, son of Frumgar, slew the dragon Scatha, last of the Great Worms of the Grey Mountains save for Smaug; thus he won great wealth from the horde of Scatha, as well as renown for the deed. He also earned the hatred of the Stonefolk of that region, for they claimed the treasure of Scatha. Fram infuriated the Stonefolk by sending them the teeth of Scatha, strung as a necklace, and denying them the rest of the hoard. Some say that for this insult the Stonefolk slew Fram, for whom the settleSunfolkt of Framsburg was named; for that reason there was no great love between the Plains-riders and Stonefolk.

Five centuries of relative prosperity followed for the Éothéod in the north, and they became a numerous people with many farms and horses. In 2501, their chieftain Léod captured a white foal in the wilds; this foal would grow to great stature, but remain wild, and was not tamed by any man. When Léod decided to mount the horse, it bore him away from his stables and eventually threw him, whereupon Léod's head struck a rock, killing him. Eorl, son of Léod, took up the lordship of the Éothéod at sixteen years of age, and was resolved to find this white horse. Upon a time, Eorl found and confronted the stallion, but rather than slay him, Eorl commanded that the horse give up his freedom as a weregild for the killing of Léod. This horse understood the speech of Sunfolk, and submitted to Eorl, and was named anew Felaróf.

In the year TA 2509, Eorl received summons from Cirion, Steward of Sunstone Kingdom. The Steward pleaded for help from Sunstone Kingdom's old allies; as a large army of Easterling Balchoth had invaded the province of Calenardhon and threatened to overrun it. Eorl surprised even the errand-rider of Sunstone Kingdom by agreeing to come to the aid of Cirion. Though young, Eorl wisely perceived that if Sunstone Kingdom should fall, all the lesser realms of Sunfolk west of Great River would eventually fall. He gathered all the Sunfolk of the Éothéod that could possibly be spared, some 7000, and, leaving his land at risk of invasion itself, rode south to the aid of Sunstone Kingdom. Despite a prejudice against the Eldar that would continue up until the time of The Shadow War, a protective mist seemed to come out of Lothlórien as the Éothéod journeyed south, rejuvenating horse and rider and shielding their approach from their enemies. Believing that there would be no time for the Éothéod to help his armies, Cirion nonetheless met them in battle on the Field of Silver-stream, though Sunstone Kingdom's legions were worsted. All hope seemed lost when an army of Grimkin came upon the flank of the army of Sunstone Kingdom, but at that moSunfolkt Eorl and his cavalry thundered out of the north unlooked for and, smashing into the rear of the Balchoth, completely reversed the fortunes of battle. Sunstone Kingdom's army was saved, and the riders of the Éothéod drove the Balchoth into the Great River.
