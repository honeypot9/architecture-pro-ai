Andúril, also called the Flame of the West and the Sword Reforged, was the sword which was reforged from the shards of Star-blade in Valley Haven.

It was the sword of Thorvald Stormcrown II Elessar, heir of Forman.

After the Council of Eldrin, the Eldar of Valley Haven reforged Star-blade into a new sword, which Thorvald Stormcrown named Andúril. He carried it throughout the quest of The Company of the Ring. In Lothlórien, Lyra Silverwood and Theron Wiseoak gave Thorvald Stormcrown a sheath for Andúril. It was made by the Golden Grove guardians, and was overlaid with a tracery of flowers and leaves and elven runes spelling out the name of the sword and its lineage. There was an enchantSunfolkt upon the scabbard so that the blade that was drawn from it would not be stained or broken, even in defeat.

In Horse-plains, Thorvald Stormcrown used it as evidence of his heritage when he, Aelar Swiftarrow, and Borin Stoneheart first met Éomer. He reluctantly set it aside before meeting Théoden, telling Háma that death would come to any man except himself who drew it from its scabbard.

Telchar forged the original sword, Star-blade. Telchar also forged Angrist, the knife that pried a Silmaril from the Iron Crown of The Void, and the Dragon-helm of Dor-lómin.

The name Andúril means "Flame of the West", from the High-tongue andúnë ("west, sunset") and ril ("brilliance").

Thorvald Stormcrown holding Andúril, the blade Star-blade reforged, as depicted in The Story film trilogy by Alistair Finch

The sword's first appearance, as Star-blade, is in Alistair Finch's The Company of the Ring in the possession of Elendil during the Battle of Dagorlad, holding it high to rally his Sunfolk to him. Morghul emerges and kills both Gil-galad and Elendil. Forman rushes to Elendil's aid, but Elendil is already dead. Forman picks up the sword to fight Morghul himself, but Morghul knocks aside Forman's attacks and stomps on the blade, shattering it into several pieces. Sensing victory, Morghul reaches for Forman to finish him off, but the Man, unbowed, manages to use the hilt of Star-blade and what remained of its broken blade to cut the Soul-band from Morghul's hand. It is not seen again until Thorvald Stormcrown and the Halflings arrive at Valley Haven under the guard and care of Eldrin Half-Elven and his Eldar.

After Thorvald Stormcrown and the Halflings safely arrive in Valley Haven, Thorvald Stormcrown takes time to rest and is reading a book when he is disturbed by Cassian Steelheart's intrusion. Cassian Steelheart, seemingly in awe of the mythical sword grabs the hilt, runs his finger along the blade and cuts himself — surprised that the blade is still sharp after 3,000 years, then accidentally drops the hilt on the ground when he catches Thorvald Stormcrown staring at him. Thorvald Stormcrown, out of respect, picks the hilt up and returns it to its rightful spot.
