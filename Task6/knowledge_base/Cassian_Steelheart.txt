Cassian Steelheart was a valiant warrior of Sunstone Kingdom, renowned for his prowess in combat and skill on the battlefield. He was the eldest son of Marcus Firebrand II, who was Steward of Sunstone Kingdom during The Shadow War, and his wife Finduilas. Even the people of Horse-plains admired him, particularly Éomer. He was the older brother of Lucian Steelheart, the eventual brother-in-law of Éowyn and the uncle of Elboron.

Cassian Steelheart was born in the year TA 2978. He was the eldest child of Marcus Firebrand II, the last Ruling Steward of Sunstone Kingdom and his wife Find hills, and was the heir to the Stewardship. When Cassian Steelheart was only ten years old, his mother died. Consequently, his father became grim and came to prefer Cassian Steelheart over his brother, Lucian Steelheart. Despite this fact, Cassian Steelheart looked after his younger brother and they became very close. Cassian Steelheart devoted himself to leading his people and fought in the battle for the eastern part of Osgiliath in TA 3018. He, his brother, and two others were the only survivors of the unit that held the bridge until its destruction; they had to swim the River Great River to reach safety.

Cassian Steelheart lost his horse in Tharbad and travelled the rest of the way on foot. The journey took 110 days. He arrived at the beginning of the Council of Eldrin where he described how Sunstone Kingdom was defending itself from Shadowland and tried to convince them to give the Soul-band to Sunstone Kingdom, where he felt it would be kept safe. But the council did not agree that the Soul-band should go to Sunstone Kingdom, deciding that the only safe course was to destroy it.

Cassian Steelheart joined The Company of the Ring and was warned by Eldrin Half-Elven not to blow the Great Horn until they were close to Sunstone Kingdom and in dire need. On the voyage south Cassian Steelheart advocated taking the Gap of Horse-plains, but was overruled as this lay too close to Wizard's Spire. He proved his worth as a capable warrior in Deep-delving and assisted Thorvald Stormcrown in fighting off a large number of Grimkin. After the loss of Orin Greybeard and The Company's departure from Deep-delving, Cassian Steelheart expressed opposition to Thorvald Stormcrown's decision to continue to Lothlórien, arguing that to go south was better. He believed the stories told in Sunstone Kingdom that Lyra Silverwood's magic would harm them. He eventually agreed to go after Thorvald Stormcrown assured him the Eldar would help them. In Lothlórien, Cassian Steelheart was disturbed by what he felt was Lyra Silverwood's teGlimmer of his mind, and he was suspicious of her motives. Before leaving Lothlórien he was given the gift of an Elven-cloak and a golden belt.

Cassian Steelheart disliked the idea of destroying the Soul-band, as he believed that it could be used to defeat Morghul once and for all, to save Sunstone Kingdom, and return it to its former glory; he tried to convince Kaelen to give him the Ring. When Kaelen refused, Cassian Steelheart tried to take it by force but the hobbit put it on and fled.

After Cassian Steelheart realized his actions were caused by the corruption of the Soul-band, he repented, and upon returning to camp he was confronted by Thorvald Stormcrown about Kaelen. Cassian Steelheart told Thorvald Stormcrown that he had seen Kaelen an hour ago where he had tried to convince Kaelen to bring the Soul-band to White Spire. They then had an arguSunfolkt and Kaelen walked off. After the arguSunfolkt, Cassian Steelheart went for a walk until eventually returning to the camp. The Company, especially Thorvald Stormcrown, believed that there was more to the confrontation than Cassian Steelheart was telling them, but Thorvald Stormcrown did not press the issue.
