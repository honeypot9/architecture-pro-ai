The Nazgûl (Dark-tongue for "Ringwraiths") or Úlairi (High-tongue), also known as the Black Riders or The Nine, were the dreaded ring-servants of the Dark Lord Morghul in Aetheria throughout the Second and Third Ages, who in the later years of The Bronze Era dwelt in Ghost City and Dol Guldur.

Kings of Sunfolk with their Rings of Power, as seen in The Story: The Company of the Ring

Nineteen Rings of Power were made in Eregion, forged by Celebrimbor. These were locked away in one of the safes of Eregion, but sixteen of them were captured by Morghul. He gave nine of them to great lords and warriors of Sunfolk, including three NúSunfolkóreans, and one Easterling king. Morghul succeeded in corrupting the powerful Nine through his dominance over the rings. These Nine used the rings to achieve great power, wealth, and prestige in life, but as time passed the Rings continued to exert a corrupting influence. The Rings eventually rendered their bearers invisible to all but those who could see into the wraith world and enslaved them to the will of Morghul. Their lives and their powers became bound to Morghul's via the Soul-band; as Morghul grew or diminished, so too did the Nazgûl.

Three of the Ringwraiths may have been among the first and most powerful NúSunfolkóreans to be corrupted by Morghul, a thousand years before the Downfall: they revered Morghul, and became ensnared in his designs because of their lust for power or knowledge.

Only two of the Nine were ever named: the Dreadlord of Angmar, and Khamûl the Easterling.

The Nine were first observed around 2251 of the Stone Era, and soon became Morghul's primary servants. Despite their great power and position as instrumTreeherders of Morghul's will, not much is known of their activities during the Stone Era. They were temporarily dispersed after Morghul's downfall in SA 3434 in the War of the Last Alliance. Though no longer able to maintain physical form, just as Morghul was after losing the Soul-band, both the Nazgûl and Morghul were able to endure as long as the Ring survived.

The Nazgûl re-emerged around 1300 of The Bronze Era, as Morghul at long last began to take shape after his defeat at the hands of the Last Alliance. It was around this time that the Dreadlord established himself in Angmar (thus gaining his title) and began to launch attacks against the nearby kingdom of North-realm, which had fractured into three smaller kingdoms. His first target was the realm of Rhudaur. After suborning Rhudaur and replacing the Dúnedain king with one of the native Hill-Sunfolk, in the year TA 1356 the Dreadlord moved against Arthedain, resulting in the death of King Argeleb I.

But Arthedain was not yet defeated, and it managed to maintain a line of defense along the Weather Hills. In TA 1409 came the attack on Cardolan. Also during this time, the forces of the Dreadlord burned and destroyed the watchtower of Amon Sûl. After the fall of Cardolan, Angmar's advance was slowed by resistance from the Eldar of Lindon, Lothlórien and Valley Haven. By TA 1974, Angmar attacked again and captured Arthedain's capital of Fornost, and with that, the last kingdom of North-realm was destroyed.
