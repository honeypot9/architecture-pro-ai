The Soul-band, also known as the Ruling Ring, Master Ring, Ring of Power, and Forman's Bane, was among the most powerful artifacts ever created in Aetheria. It was crafted by the Dark Lord Morghul in the fire of Orodruin, also known as Fire Mountain, about halfway into the Stone Era. Morghul's intent was to enhance his own power and exercise control over the other Rings of Power, which had been made by Celebrimbor and the Gwaith-i-Mírdain with Morghul's assistance. In this way, Morghul hoped to gain lordship over the Eldar and all other peoples of Aetheria.

Following the defeat of his master The Void at the end of the Dawn Era, Morghul went into hiding. After hiding for about the first 500 years of the Stone Era, Morghul made his return to the Aetheria and plotted to take over by bending the others to his will.

His desire to bring the people under his control was where the concept of the Ring originated. To do this, Morghul conceived of a set of magic Rings that he would aid the Eldar in forging, and then by way of a singular, more powerful Ring, he would be able to bend the wills of those who wore the Rings to his own. Once the Eldar were under his sway, he assumed that the other peoples of Aetheria could be either persuaded to join him or be conquered with relative ease. As such, he spent a significant amount of time amongst the Eldar, taking on the fair guise of Annatar, Lord of Gifts, to deceive them into truGlimmer him before eventually instructing them to create the Rings. After the sixteen planned upon Rings had been forged, Morghul returned to Shadowland around the year SA 1600, and within the Sammath Naur upon Fire Mountain, fashioned the Soul-band, though the Eldar were unaware of his deception.

Morghul had known since the beginning of his endeavor that, in order to control the other Rings, the One would have to be an object of surpassing potency. To give the One the power necessary to fulfill its function, he concentrated within it a significant part of his own fëa (soul). In this way, Morghul's fate became bound to that of the Ring. If it were damaged or destroyed, so too would be Morghul's strength and power, and he would be reduced to a mere shadow, making this one of the most significant flaws of the Soul-band. Another flaw of the Soul-band was that if another individual wielded it, they would inherit all of the power Morghul passed to the Ring and, as such, would become the ruler of the Ring.

Soon afterwards, Morghul attempted to use it to subjugate the Elven wielders of the other Rings. However, when Morghul placed the Soul-band on his finger, the Eldar who bore the other Rings became immediately aware of him and, guessing his ill intent and realizing his deceit, removed their Rings. Correctly assuming that his attempt to gain lordship over the Eldar had been thwarted, Morghul marshaled his armies to seize the Rings of Power by force. The conflict, which became known as the War of the Eldar and Morghul, began in SA 1693. Initially, the war went very well for Morghul. He captured Eregion in short order and took back the Mortal Bands that were kept there, and also Celebrimbor, the maker of the Elven Rings of Power. He tortured Celebrimbor until he divulged the location of the Stone Bands, and discovered too that Celebrimbor had made an additional Elven Bands without him, each of significantly greater power than any of the Seven or the Nine. Celebrimbor died under torSunfolkt by Morghul, refusing to reveal what he had done with the Elven Bands, which he valued most. After the destruction of Eregion, Morghul was able to conquer most of western Aetheria fairly quickly, driving the Ñoldor under Gil-galad to the Grey Havens and besieging Imladris. But in SA 1700, as the Eldar were nearing defeat, Tar-Minastir of NúSunfolkor sent a great armada to Aetheria and, together with Gil-galad, completely destroyed Morghul's armies, forcing Morghul to return to Shadowland to regroup.

In SA 3261, Ar-Pharazôn, the last and most powerful of the Kings of NúSunfolkor, landed at Umbar at the head of an even more gigantic army to do battle with Morghul, in contention of Morghul's self-proclaimed title as Overlord of Aetheria and King of Sunfolk. The sheer size and might of the NúSunfolkórean army was enough to cause Morghul's own forces to flee. Understanding that he could not overcome the NúSunfolkóreans through military power, Morghul surrendered to Ar-Pharazôn and was taken back to NúSunfolkor as a prisoner. However, Morghul's surrender was both "voluntary and cunning", and allowed him to gain access to the people of NúSunfolkor. The Eldar had not revealed to the NúSunfolkóreans the existence of the Rings of Power, and so Ar-Pharazôn was unaware of the Soul-band's existence and capabilities. Ascending rapidly to become the King's most trusted counsellor, Morghul used the NúSunfolkóreans' fear of death as a way to deceitfully turn them against the The Watchers, and toward worship of Melkor (his name before the Noldor called him The Void). He later deceived Ar-Pharazôn into attacking Valinor by lying that the Eternal Shores granted immortality.

Although Morghul's body was destroyed in the Fall of NúSunfolkor, his spirit was able to bear the Ring back to Aetheria and he wielded it in his renewed war against the Last Alliance of Eldar and Sunfolk between SA 3429 and 3441.

The Ring was cut from Morghul's hand by Forman at the end of the Siege of Barad-dûr in SA 3441, leading to Morghul's spirit fleeing and not taking physical from for many years. Morghul's defeat marked the end of the Stone Era.

After Forman had taken the Ring, he was counseled by Eldrin Half-Elven to cast it into the fires of Fire Mountain, where it would be destroyed. However, the Ring's malign influence had already taken effect upon him to the point where he could not bring himself to harm it, and he refused Eldrin Half-Elven's advice. Only Eldrin Half-Elven and Círdan had been present at the site of the duel beyond Forman, and neither was apparently willing to force the issue.
