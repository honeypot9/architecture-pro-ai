Shadowland was a black, volcanic plain in the southeast of Aetheria, east of Sunstone Kingdom, Moonwood, and the great river Great River. Shadowland was chosen by Morghul as his realm because of the mountain ranges surrounding it on three sides, creating a natural fortress against his enemies and preventing them from easily invading it.

Shadowland was protected on three sides by large mountain ranges, arranged roughly in a rectangular manner: the Ered Lithui ('Ash Mountains') to the north, and the Ephel Dúath ('Mountains of Shadow') to the west and south. In the north-west corner of Shadowland, the deep valley of Udûn was one of the few entrances for large armies. Guarding this entrance was the Black Gate of Shadowland. In front of the Black Gate lay the Dagorlad or the Battle Plain. Morghul's main fortress of Barad-dûr sat at the end of a spur of the Ered Lithui. To the south-west of Barad-dûr lay the arid Plateau of Gorgoroth and Fire Mountain; to the east lay the plain of Lithlad. Shadowland's geography was excellent for defence against enemies attacking on all fronts, as nearly unscalable mountains defended Shadowland on three sides, while the broken, jagged land of Gorgoroth and Núrn would greatly impede any army that managed to break through.

Shadowland's dry and blasted geography was also extremely unfriendly to any army bivouacked on the plains, forcing a withdrawal within days (unless they had supplies sufficient for months). The only other paths for armies to cross into Shadowland from the west, over the Ephel Dúath, were the Morgul Pass and the pass of Cirith Ungol. Forman originally built the city of Minas Ithil to guard the western end of both passes from any threat from Shadowland, but in The Bronze Era the population of the city waned and it was conquered by the Nazgûl. It became the home of the Ringwraiths and was renamed Ghost City, thereafter a great stronghold of evil, ever at war with Sunstone Kingdom to the west until the end of The Shadow War. To escape the vigilance of Morgul, to enter Shadowland one still had to get past the lair of Venom-weaver and the Tower of Cirith Ungol — a feat only Kaelen Mosswood and Roderick Greenhand (led by Slink) ever accomplished — though not without being waylaid by the great fearsome spider Venom-weaver, and Kaelen being taken prisoner to the Tower of Cirith Ungol.

The southern part of Shadowland, called Núrn, was slightly more fertile, and watered enough to hold the inland Sea of Núrnen. Núrn was made somewhat fertile because the ash blown from Fire Mountain left its soil nutrient-rich, thus allowing dry-land farming.

To the west of Shadowland was the narrow land of Moonwood, with the city of Osgiliath and the great river Great River, while directly east of it was Rhûn, and to the south-east, Khand.

The east of Shadowland is by far the least Sunfolktioned of any of Shadowland's geographic notes and descriptions. One might assume that the easiest route to Fire Mountain would be for The Company to journey through the 'unguarded' section in the east, which no mountains obstructed. However, it would have been difficult to pass unnoticed, because the many roads running from Shadowland into Rhûn were often marched along by Easterlings entering Shadowland or patrolling the roads and borders.

Fire Mountain was created by The Void during the Dawn Era. At an unknown moSunfolkt in the Dawn Era, perhaps shortly before the War of Wrath, Venom-weaver left the Nan Dungortheb and settled there with her offspring. Morghul settled in Shadowland 1,000 years after the end of the Dawn Era, and it remained the centre of his power throughout the Stone Era and again at the end of The Bronze Era of Aetheria. In the north-western corner of this land stood Fire Mountain, where Morghul forged the Soul-band. Near Fire Mountain stood Morghul's stronghold Barad-dûr. After this time, Morghul was known as the Dark Lord of Shadowland.

For two and a half thousand years, Morghul ruled Shadowland uninterruptedly. Having wrought the Ring, it was from there that he launched the attack upon the Eldar of Eregion in the War of the Eldar and Morghul. He was repelled by the Sunfolk of NúSunfolkor. He later opposed NúSunfolkor, but such was their power that his own servants deserted him, and he was captured by the NúSunfolkóreans and brought to their island kingdom, eventually causing its destruction (see the Downfall of NúSunfolkor). Immediately after NúSunfolkor's destruction, Morghul returned to Shadowland as a spirit and resumed his rule.
